About the Campaign🐾

The Pet-Friendly City Campaign is an initiative to make urban areas safer, more inclusive, and friendly for our furry friends.
We aim to raise awareness, encourage adoption, suppert stary animal welfare, and build a compassionate community of volunteers and citizens.

From installing pet water stations to organizing awareness drives and adoptions camps, our volunteers are the backbone of this movement.
Join us in turning every city street into a paw-some space!🐶🐾

Impact Stories That Inspire 🐾

🐶 🐾 1. Ammu – The One-Eyed Warrior Story:

Ammu was a stray pup hit by a bike on Bandar Road in Vijayawada. Local college students rescued her and rushed her to Helping Hands for Animals. Unfortunately, she lost one eye, but thanks to the care and love she received, she made a full recovery. Now, she lives at the shelter and welcomes every new dog like family.
Inspires: Rescue, rehabilitation, and love beyond appearance.

💪🐾 Meera – The Street Savior

Meera, a 20-year-old engineering student from Guntur, started feeding 5 stray dogs near her PG. Over 2 years, she vaccinated 40+ animals, created awareness posters, and worked with the local vet to organize a sterilization drive. Her entire street is now stray-friendly and clean.
Inspires: Youth activism and female leadership in pet welfare.

👮‍♂️🐾 Ramu – The Police Pawtrol

Ramu started following constables in Vizag’s MVP Colony. He was never driven away. Instead, officers fed him, got him vaccinated, and even gave him a collar. He now guards the gates and is known by all the locals. He’s even part of Republic Day parades!
Inspires: Public institution embracing stray animals.
